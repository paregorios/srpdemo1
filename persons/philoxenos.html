ܦܝܠܠܘܟܣܝܢܘܣ ܕܡܒܘܓ (Philoxenos of Mabbug)

ca. 440s?-523

Metropolitan bp. of Mabbug (modern Membij/ancient Hierapolis) (485-519), ascetic theologian, christological polemicist, and sponsor of the Philoxenian NT.

Geography

  • Place of birth: Garmakan/Beth Garmai (ca. 440s?)
  • Place of study: Edessa/Antiochia ad Callirhoem

Biography

  • 484: Supporting the Henotikon, Philoxenos visited Constantinople in 484 to offer Zeno a Miaphysite profession of faith.
  • 0486: After the restoration of Peter the Fuller as patriarch of Antioch, Philoxenos was named metropolitan of Mabbug.
  • 512: Philoxenos’s anti-Chalcedon efforts culminated in 512 when Flavian was ousted in favor of Severus as Patr. of Antioch. Philoxenos and his suffragan bishops (including a relation, Philoxenos of Doliche) assisted at Severus’s consecration.
  • 519: In 519, Justin I exiled Philoxenos. In his final years, Philoxenos continued writing to monastic communities, encouraging them to persist in ascesis and Miaphysite christology.
  • 523: Philoxenos died in 523 under house arrest. Later traditions that he was executed are difficult to substantiate.
