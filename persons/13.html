ܐܦܪܝܡ (Ephrem)

306-373

Ephrem was a Christian teacher and prolific writer of the 4th century C.E. in Syria and northern Mesopotamia. He is a key author in the Syriac traditions and revered as a saint in a wide variety of Christian churches.

Geography

  • Place of birth: Nisibis/Antiochia 306-359 — Born probably of Christian parents in the early years of the 4th cent. in the region of Nisibis, he spent most of his life in Nisibis, serving a series of bishops (beginning with Yaʿqub of Nisibis, d. 338).
  • Place of death: Edessa/Antiochia ad Callirhoem 363-June 09, 373
  • Ad Tygrem/Amida 359-363 — Ephrem stayed in Amida temporarily after the Persians gained control of Nisibis.
