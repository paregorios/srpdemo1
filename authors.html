  1. ܓܪܝܓܘܪܝܘܣ ܒܪ ܥܒܪܝܐ (Bar ʿEbroyo, Grigorios)

    Maph. (since 1264) and polymath. Foremost representative of the Syriac Renaissance of the 12th–13th cent.

  2. ܐܠܝܐ ܕܨܘܒܐ (Eliya of Nisibis)

    Metropolitan of Nisibis and author.

  3. ܐܦܪܝܡ (Ephrem)

    Ephrem was a Christian teacher and prolific writer of the 4th century C.E. in Syria and northern Mesopotamia. He is a key author in the Syriac traditions and revered as a saint in a wide variety of Christian churches.

  4. ܓܪܝܓܘܪܝܘܣ ܕܢܐܙܝܢܙܘ (Gregory of Nazianzus)

    Greek Church Father, and one of the three Cappadocian Fathers, whose influential writings earned him the title ‘the theologian’.

  5. ܦܝܠܠܘܟܣܝܢܘܣ ܕܡܒܘܓ (Philoxenos of Mabbug)

    Metropolitan bp. of Mabbug (modern Membij/ancient Hierapolis) (485-519), ascetic theologian, christological polemicist, and sponsor of the Philoxenian NT.
